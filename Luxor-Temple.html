Luxor Temple

The sacred sanctuary of ancient Thebes

About Luxor Temple

Luxor Temple is a large Ancient Egyptian temple complex located on the east bank of the Nile River in the city today known as Luxor (ancient Thebes). Constructed approximately 1400 BCE, the temple was dedicated to the Theban Triad of Amun, Mut, and Chons and was the focus of the annual Opet Festival.

Unlike other temples in Thebes, Luxor Temple is not dedicated to a cult god or a deified version of the pharaoh in death. Instead, it is dedicated to the rejuvenation of kingship; it may have been where many of the pharaohs of Egypt were crowned in reality or conceptually.

Quick Facts

  • Location: East bank of Nile, Luxor, Egypt
  • Built: 1400 BCE (New Kingdom)
  • Constructed by: Amenhotep III and Ramses II
  • Length: 190 meters (623 ft)
  • Width: 55 meters (180 ft)
  • Key Features: Great Pylon, Colonnade, Sun Court
  • UNESCO Status: Part of Ancient Thebes World Heritage Site

Visiting Luxor Temple

Luxor Temple is open daily from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. The temple is particularly spectacular at night when it's beautifully illuminated, creating a magical atmosphere. Early morning visits are recommended to avoid the heat and crowds.

The entrance fee includes access to the entire temple complex. Audio guides are available for rent at the entrance, and licensed tour guides can be hired on site. The temple is wheelchair accessible, though some areas may be challenging due to ancient thresholds.

Combine your visit with the nearby Luxor Museum for a deeper understanding of the temple's history and significance. Many Nile cruise packages include guided tours of Luxor Temple as part of their itinerary.
